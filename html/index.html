What is Hackaton?

A hackathon is an event that brings together experts in a collaborative environment to solve a specific problem. While most hackathons focus on computer programming and IT, other industries also organize them. Participants work on creating proofs of concept, minimal viable products, and innovative solutions within a limited timeframe.

Why Hackaton is needed?

Hackathons are dynamic events that bring together experts from various fields to collaborate on solving specific problems within a limited timeframe. These intense environments foster creativity, innovation, and real-world experience. Participants work as a team, share knowledge, and build practical solutions. Hackathons also provide networking opportunities, portfolio-building experiences, and a chance to address social issues. In summary, hackathons steer innovation, build confidence, and offer a platform for collaborative learning and creativity

How Hackaton is conducted?

Hackathons are dynamic events that bring together experts from various fields to collaborate on solving specific problems within a limited timeframe. Participants meet and greet, pitch their ideas, form teams, and work on turning these concepts into working prototypes or demos using programming languages and other tools. At the end of the event, teams present their finished or partially completed projects, which are evaluated by a jury. Hackathons foster creativity, innovation, real-world experience, networking, and portfolio-building opportunities. In summary, they steer innovation, build confidence, and offer a platform for collaborative learning and creativity.
